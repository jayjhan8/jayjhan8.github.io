First Post!

2018-03-22

Welcome everyone. Thanks for stopping by my blog. This is very new to me, so I’m not quite sure what to write now. I decided to try something new given the recent turn of events in my life. As many of you know, I was recently diagnosed with stage 3 testicular cancer. Before you get so wrapped up about the word “cancer,” let me tell you how it all happened.

Mid-December of 2017, I found out that I had a tiny lump underneath my right nipple. I dismissed the lump for a good few weeks just as I have done with any other temporary skin bumps or scars. Interestingly, I had someone tell me few years back that I may get breasts from consuming foods with high estrogen content such as grapefruits and tofu. I found the comment ridiculous then, but I couldn’t stop thinking it could be a possibility given my liking for tofu. I love tofu. Few weeks passed, and the lump did not reduce in size, so I figured going to see a doctor wouldn’t hurt. The internist deemed the lump as gynecomastia. It occurs to many teenagers going through puberty and aging men. I didn’t think too much about it as long as it wasn’t life-threatening, and I don’t really mind another cycle of puberty, either – A few more inches could help me acheive my OG childhood dream in becoming the next basketball star. The internist had recommended that I get an ultrasound on my testicles as it may be excreting more estrogen in my body than usual. Again without thinking too much, I went ahead and did just that.

The next day is when I hear about Testicular Cancer for the very first time. Everything happened pretty quickly afterwards – I met with the urologist, scheduled a surgery to remove the cancerous testicle, and told my parents. As I wait for the pathology results, I did some research on testicular cancer. Some promising words – “If any cancer, this is the one you want.” It has some abnormally high cure rates in the 90%, and most often times it’s either in stage 1 or 2. At this point, I’m thinking to myself “I’m not exhibiting any of the later stage symptoms, I’m going to be alright.”

I knew something was off the moment I saw the nurse at the urology clinic. Heaviness in her greeting tone and a sullen expression on her face. I go in and my urologist says I am in stage 3 and I need to go see an oncologist for treatment. This is when I thought, “Dang, I’m in for a long journey.” My parents flew in shortly afterwards and now I’m preparing for a chemotherapy treatment starting this Sunday. Over the course of the next 3 or 4 months, I’ll be sharing my day-to-day journey in chemo. This is a way for me to communicate to others on how I am doing, but I also share in hopes to encourage you in your own battle. Whatever that may be, I hope God is guiding and strengthening you along that journey as He will be in mine.
