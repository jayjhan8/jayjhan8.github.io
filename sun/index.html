Sun

2018-05-02

Seattle is one of the most beautiful places to live in. It took me a while to get used to the winter drag, but I’ve adapted and learned to appreciate the summer. The sun’s out today, birds are chirping, and the bees are zapping away – summer’s just around the corner. On my rooftop, I can look far out into the horizon to the South, where there are so many trees and other plants interspersed around buildings. What a blessing to be a part of this PNW summer! I went for a run today for the first time in a long while. I’m not sure if I’m completely out of shape or it’s that the chemo has taken a toll on me, but my lungs couldn’t keep up after an easy jog. That’s okay, I have to start building myself up slowly, and I’ll be ready for a marathon soon enough. Maybe. We’ll see. Regardless, I enjoy running because I think clearly and freely. It’s one way I destress and find peace. Thanks to you all who have been commenting on the blog! Your words are encouraging to me, and I find joy and courage in the encouragements. D-1 until the scan!
